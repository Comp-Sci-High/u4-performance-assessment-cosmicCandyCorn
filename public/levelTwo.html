Intermediate Breakfast Ideas

Cheese & Veggie Omelette

Cost: $$

How to Make:

  • Whisk eggs with salt, pepper, and a splash of milk.
  • Heat butter in a pan and pour the eggs.
  • Add cheese, bell peppers, onions, and spinach.
  • Fold the omelette once it sets.
  • Serve warm with toast.

Fun Fact: Omelettes have been a breakfast staple since ancient Persia!

Belgian Waffles

Cost: $$

How to Make:

  • Mix flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt.
  • Whisk eggs, milk, butter, and vanilla in a separate bowl.
  • Combine wet and dry ingredients.
  • Pour batter into a waffle iron and cook until golden.
  • Top with syrup, fruit, or whipped cream.

Fun Fact: Belgian waffles are fluffier and deeper than regular waffles!

French Toast

Cost: $$

How to Make:

  • Whisk eggs, milk, cinnamon, and vanilla.
  • Dip bread slices into the mixture.
  • Heat butter in a pan and cook bread on both sides.
  • Sprinkle with powdered sugar.
  • Serve with syrup or fruit.

Fun Fact: French toast dates back to the Roman Empire!

  • Add tomatoes and spices (paprika, cumin, salt).

Fun Fact: Shakshuka is a popular North African and Middle Eastern dish!

Eggs Benedict

Cost: $$

How to Make:

  • Poach eggs in simmering water.
  • Toast English muffins and top with ham.
  • Make hollandaise sauce (butter, egg yolk, lemon juice).
  • Place poached eggs on muffins.
  • Drizzle with hollandaise sauce and serve.

Fun Fact: Eggs Benedict was created in New York in the 1860s!

Crepes

Cost: $$

How to Make:

  • Whisk flour, eggs, milk, and melted butter.
  • Heat a pan and pour a thin layer of batter.
  • Cook for 1-2 minutes per side.
  • Fill with fruit, Nutella, or cheese.
  • Fold and serve warm.

Fun Fact: Crepes originated in Brittany, France!
