Easy & Affordable Breakfast Ideas

Avocado Toast

Cost: $

How to Make:

  • Toast a slice of bread.
  • Mash half an avocado with salt & pepper.
  • Spread the mashed avocado on toast.
  • Drizzle with olive oil or lemon juice.
  • Top with optional ingredients like eggs or tomatoes.

Fun Fact: Avocado toast became a global trend in the 2010s, especially popular in brunch culture!

Yogurt Parfait

Cost: $

How to Make:

  • Layer Greek yogurt into a cup.
  • Add a layer of granola.
  • Top with fresh fruits like berries.
  • Drizzle honey or maple syrup.
  • Repeat layers if desired and serve chilled.

Fun Fact: Parfaits originated in France and mean "perfect" in French!

Banana Pancakes

Cost: $

How to Make:

  • Mash one ripe banana in a bowl.
  • Add one egg and mix well.
  • Heat a pan and grease it lightly.
  • Pour small amounts of batter and cook both sides.
  • Serve with honey or peanut butter.

Fun Fact: Jack Johnson made banana pancakes famous with his song “Banana Pancakes.”

Oatmeal Bowl

Cost: $

How to Make:

  • Boil 1/2 cup oats with water or milk.
  • Simmer until thickened.
  • Add cinnamon or vanilla for flavor.
  • Top with bananas, berries, or nuts.
  • Drizzle honey or maple syrup before serving.

Fun Fact: Oats are one of the healthiest grains and can reduce cholesterol!

Scrambled Eggs

Cost: $

How to Make:

  • Crack two eggs into a bowl and whisk.
  • Heat a pan and add butter.
  • Pour eggs into the pan and stir gently.
  • Cook until soft and fluffy.
  • Season with salt, pepper, and optional cheese.

Fun Fact: Scrambled eggs have been around for thousands of years, dating back to ancient Persia!

Peanut Butter Toast

Cost: $

How to Make:

  • Toast a slice of bread.
  • Spread a generous layer of peanut butter.
  • Top with banana slices or honey.
  • Sprinkle with cinnamon for extra flavor.
  • Enjoy with a glass of milk!

Fun Fact: Peanut butter was first patented in 1884 by Marcellus Gilmore Edson.
